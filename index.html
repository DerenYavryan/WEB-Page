How to Build Your Computer

Follow these steps to assemble your own custom PC. Take your time and work carefully!

1

Prepare Your Workspace

Clear a large, clean surface. Use an anti-static mat if possible. Ground yourself by touching a metal object to discharge static electricity. Gather your tools: Phillips screwdriver, zip ties for cable management, and thermal paste (usually comes with CPU cooler).

2

Install the CPU

Open the CPU socket on the motherboard by lifting the retention arm. Align the CPU with the socket (look for the golden triangle or notches). Gently place it inâ€”never force it. Lower the retention arm to secure it. The CPU should drop in effortlessly if aligned correctly.

3

Install the CPU Cooler

Apply thermal paste (a pea-sized dot in the center of the CPU, or use pre-applied paste). Attach the cooler according to manufacturer instructionsâ€”this varies by model. Connect the cooler's fan cable to the CPU_FAN header on the motherboard.

4

Install RAM

Open the RAM slot clips on both ends. Align the notch on the RAM stick with the slot. Press down firmly until both clips snap into place. For dual-channel configuration, check your motherboard manual for correct slot placement (usually slots 2 and 4).

5

Install M.2 SSD (if applicable)

Locate the M.2 slot on your motherboard. Remove the mounting screw, insert the SSD at a 30-degree angle into the slot, then press down and secure with the screw. Some motherboards have a heatsinkâ€”attach it if included.

6

Install Motherboard I/O Shield & Standoffs

Snap the I/O shield (the metal plate that came with your motherboard) into the case from the inside. Install motherboard standoffs (brass spacers) in the case at positions matching your motherboard's screw holes. This prevents shorts.

7

Mount the Motherboard

Carefully lower the motherboard into the case, aligning the I/O ports with the shield and screw holes with standoffs. Screw it in gentlyâ€”don't overtighten. Work in a star pattern to ensure even pressure.

8

Install the Power Supply (PSU)

Place the PSU in its bay (usually bottom-rear of case). Orient the fan correctlyâ€”typically facing down if there's a vent, or facing into the case if not. Secure with four screws from the back of the case.

9

Install the GPU

Remove the necessary PCIe slot covers from the case. Align the GPU with the top PCIe x16 slot (closest to CPU). Press down firmly until it clicks. Secure the GPU bracket to the case with screws. Connect PCIe power cables from PSU if required.

10

Install Storage Drives

Mount SSDs in 2.5" bays or HDD in 3.5" bays using screws or tool-less brackets. Connect SATA data cables to the motherboard and SATA power cables from the PSU. Ensure cables are routed neatly.

11

Connect Front Panel & Case Cables

Connect power button, reset button, power LED, and HDD LED to the motherboard headers (check manual for pinout). Connect USB headers, audio headers, and any RGB/fan controllers. Connect case fans to motherboard or fan hub.

12

Connect Power Cables

Connect the 24-pin ATX power to motherboard, 8-pin (or 4+4 pin) CPU power near the CPU socket, PCIe power to GPU (if needed), and SATA/Molex power to drives. Double-check all connections are secure.

13

Cable Management

Route cables through the back panel of the case. Use zip ties or velcro straps to bundle cables neatly. Good cable management improves airflow and aesthetics. Hide excess cables behind the motherboard tray.

14

Final Check & First Boot

Double-check all connections. Ensure no cables obstruct fans. Close the case. Connect monitor to GPU (not motherboard), keyboard, and mouse. Plug in the power cable, flip the PSU switch, and press the power button. If it boots to BIOS, congratulations! Install your operating system.
